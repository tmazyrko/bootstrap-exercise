Disk Encryption

Encrypting your computer's disk(s) is a surefire way to add an extra, strong layer of protection around your personal files. Anyone who obtains your device won't be able to read the data on your encrypted drives, and most boot sector malware would also be rendered ineffective. If you are using Windows, the best disk encryption solution is one called BitLocker, and it's already built into the OS. BitLocker is a fairly mature encryption technology that works by encrypting entire drives, but perhaps its most distinguishing feature is just how simple and seamlessly integrated into Windows it is. Generally, using BitLocker comes at no cost; the performance penalty that comes with full-disk disk encryption has been minimized by modern hardware like SSDs. Plus, when encrypting you system drive, you generally won't even need to go through any extra steps to unlock your computer—simply entering your Windows password will let BitLocker unlock your drive.

For the MacOS users among us, Apple offers a similar integrated solution known as FileVault. The encryption occurs on the fly, in the background as you use your Mac. Like BitLocker (unless otherwise configured), FileVault uses AES-128 encryption in XTS mode with a 256-bit key, and both tools are compliant with the US Government's Federal Information Processing Standards (FIPS).
