Password Security

Strong passwords are the mortar to the bricks of your online security—without them, everything will fall apart and you will find your accounts hacked sooner or later. However, as the complexity of a password increases, the convenience decreases. It becomes harder to remember and many people resort to writing it down, which defeats the purpose of a strong password entirely! Fortunately, you can use a password manager to make your password stronger, and your life easier.

Think of a password manager like a notebook for storing all of your passwords, secured by a master password than only you know. It may seem counter-intuitive to entrust your various passwords to a single master password, but assuming you've chosen a strong and unique, yet memorable, master password that you don't use anywhere else, then you are setting yourself up for success.

Password managers can also generate and save strong, complex, and unique password when you sign up for new accounts, ensuring that you are not reusing passwords or accidentally creating weak ones on your own. There are plenty of options available online, but I personally use Bitwarden due to its free and open-source nature. Check it out at bitwarden.com.

You can visit a website like howsecureismypassword.net to test your current password.
