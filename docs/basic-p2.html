Search Engines

Today, Google Search processes over 65,000 search queries per second—this is no surprise seeing how Google is known for it's top-class search results and has become a household name. However, using Google (and other common search engines like Yahoo or Bing) comes at a price: your privacy. It only takes a quick glance through Google's data privacy policies to see that they save things like your search queries and IP addresses to build a profile on each and every one of their users. This profile is used to personalize your search results, sure, but it's also used to target advertisements and is even sold to third-parties.

Fortunately, recent years have seen an increase in options for privacy-first search engines. DuckDuckGo is probably the most well known example with about 60,000,000 daily searches. The company was founded in 2008, and uses a compilation of over 400 sources to return search results all while not profiling any of its users. However, my personal private search engine of choice is Startpage. They pay Google to use their search API, effectively acting as a privacy buffer between you and Google's servers. In other words, you still get Google's class-leading search results, just without the tracking. Additionally, unlike DuckDuckGo which is headquartered in the US (a country notorious for it's abysmal privacy laws), Startpage is headquartered in the Netherlands where privacy laws are among the most stringent in the world.
